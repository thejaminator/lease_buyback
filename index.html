Important Restrictions After LBS

  • Cannot sell flat on the open market after LBS completion (T&C 13.1)
  • Cannot rent out whole flat — only spare bedrooms (except 1 & 2-room), subject to HDB rules (T&C 14.1)
  • 5-year wait before applying for another subsidised HDB flat (T&C 17.1)
  • 2.5-year wait from surrender/transfer of LBS flat before applying for HDB rental flat (T&C 17.2)
  • Private property: must complete 5-year MOP (10-year for Plus/Prime) and continue to stay in HDB flat (T&C 19.1)
  • Irrevocable: once legal documents are signed, the lease cannot be reverted (T&C 12.2)
  • Early termination: HDB may allow premature surrender; residual value based on straight-line depreciation (T&C 16.1-16.2)
  • CPF LIFE continues even if flat is returned to HDB prematurely (T&C 16.3)

This calculator provides estimates only. Actual amounts depend on HDB's valuation and CPF Board's assessment. Flat owners bear their own legal costs (T&C 3.1). Please consult HDB for the official computation.

Key Terms

FRS — Full Retirement Sum
The CPF savings needed in your Retirement Account (RA) to receive higher monthly payouts under CPF LIFE. Sole owners must top up to the age-adjusted FRS. For 2026: $220,400 (age 65–69), $210,400 (age 70–79), $200,400 (age 80+).
BRS — Basic Retirement Sum
Half of the FRS. For households with 2 or more owners, each owner tops up to the age-adjusted BRS. For 2026: $110,200 (age 65–69), $105,200 (age 70–79), $100,200 (age 80+).
RA — Retirement Account
A CPF account created at age 55 by combining savings from your Special Account and Ordinary Account. Your RA savings are used for retirement payouts under CPF LIFE or the Retirement Sum Scheme.
CPF LIFE
A national annuity scheme that provides monthly payouts for life. Owners below 80 with at least $60,000 in their RA after the LBS top-up must join CPF LIFE. The entire RA balance is used as premium.
RSS — Retirement Sum Scheme
For owners aged 80+ who are not eligible for CPF LIFE. Provides monthly payouts from the RA until the balance is depleted (not lifelong).
LBS Bonus
A cash bonus from HDB based on flat type and total CPF top-up amount. Full bonus if total top-up ≥ $60,000; otherwise pro-rated. Up to $30,000 (3-room), $15,000 (4-room), $7,500 (5-room).
Bala's Table (Lease Depreciation Curve)
Used to estimate the value of the tail-end lease sold to HDB. Leasehold property does not lose value in a straight line — it depreciates slowly in early years and more steeply as the lease shortens. Bala's Table, originally derived by property valuer Bala Murugan, maps remaining lease years to a percentage of freehold value. For example, a 65-year lease retains about 85% of freehold value, while a 30-year lease retains about 49%. This calculator uses these factors to estimate sale proceeds: Proceeds = Market Value × (Factor(remaining) − Factor(retained)) / Factor(remaining).
Manner of Holding
Joint Tenancy: Equal ownership (50/50). Proceeds disbursed as a single cheque in joint names. Tenancy-in-Common: Ownership in specified shares. Proceeds split and disbursed as separate cheques.

Terms & Conditions Reference

1. Approval Requirements

  • All flat owners and spouses must satisfy all prevailing T&C
  • If flat announced for redevelopment, eligibility assessed as at announcement date
  • Persons on debarment list are not eligible
  • Spouse's consent required even if not listed as owner
  • All owners, spouses, and witnesses must attend appointments
  • All debts, arrears, taxes, and fees must be settled before approval

2. Net Proceeds & CPF Top-up

  • Apportionment: Net proceeds split based on manner of holding (50/50 for joint tenancy, respective shares for tenancy-in-common)
  • Step 1: Each owner tops up own RA to specified requirement (FRS for sole owner, BRS for 2+ owners)
  • Step 2: If insufficient, top up with entire share of proceeds
  • Step 3: If combined remaining proceeds exceed $100,000, the excess is used for further RA top-up to current FRS
  • Step 4: Remaining proceeds disbursed as cash (single cheque for joint tenancy, separate cheques for tenancy-in-common)
  • MA top-up option: If all owners met FRS and total top-ups are below $60,000, owners can opt to top up MAs and/or spouses' RAs/MAs to reach $60,000 total for full bonus
  • CPF LIFE: Owners below 80 with RA ≥ $60,000 after top-up must join CPF LIFE. Entire RA used as premium.
  • Retirement Sum Scheme: Owners aged 80+ receive monthly payouts from RA until depleted

2.5 LBS Bonus

  • Full bonus if total RA/MA top-ups ≥ $60,000: up to $30,000 (3-room), $15,000 (4-room), $7,500 (5-room)
  • Pro-rated if less: $1 per $2 (3-room), $1 per $4 (4-room), $1 per $8 (5-room)
  • Shared among eligible SC owners & SC spouses based on individual top-up amounts
  • One-time only per household
  • No recovery if flat returned prematurely
  • No bonus if all owners already at FRS before joining LBS

3-6. Payments, Upgrading & Levies

  • Flat owners bear own legal costs and expenses
  • CPF monies may be used for legal costs (subject to CPF rules)
  • If upgrading programme affects flat, prevailing policies apply
  • LBS completed before polling day: no upgrading cost needed
  • LBS completed on/after polling day: upgrading cost withheld from proceeds
  • 10% upgrading levy on tail-end lease value if owner enjoyed 2+ upgrading subsidies
  • Resale levy applies if buying another subsidised flat

7-10. Ownership & Flat Conditions

  • Manner of holding remains same after LBS completion
  • No change of flat owner/occupier during application
  • Must allow HDB/valuers entry to assess flat
  • Title must be in order; replacement title deed needed if lost

11-12. Cancellation & Completion

  • HDB cancels on written request from owners
  • HDB may reject/cancel for false declaration, missing documents, non-compliance, or ineligibility
  • Cancellation fees (conveyancing, stamp, registration) still payable
  • Service/conservancy charges and property tax must be paid up to completion
  • Transaction is irrevocable after legal documents are signed

13-19. Post-LBS Restrictions

  • Cannot sell LBS flat on open market
  • Cannot rent out whole flat (bedrooms only, except 1 & 2-room)
  • CPF refund required on surrender/transfer/disposal of flat
  • Early termination: residual value based on straight-line depreciation
  • CPF LIFE continues even if flat returned prematurely
  • 5-year wait for new subsidised HDB flat
  • 2.5-year wait for HDB rental flat (from surrender/transfer)
  • Private property purchase: 5-year MOP (10 for Plus/Prime), must continue living in HDB flat

20-22. Other Conditions

  • Compulsory acquisition/revesting for breach of HDA provisions
  • Must pay all rates, taxes, charges
  • No transfer/assignment/mortgage without HDB consent
  • Must keep flat in good repair
  • Must obtain HDB consent before alterations
  • Consent given to HDB, CPFB, IRAS for use of personal information

Terms valid as of Dec 2025. Subject to change. HDB's decisions are final. False declarations may result in fines up to $5,000 and/or imprisonment up to 6 months.
